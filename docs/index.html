KG-RAG vs Flat RAG

multi-hop QA · 2WikiMultiHopQA · 100 held-out questions · pre-computed, no live model

Does a knowledge graph help multi-hop RAG? The honest answer.

The first version of this experiment showed a large multi-hop win (+8.8 token-F1) for adding a knowledge-graph retrieval leg. But that graph was answer-enriched — built only from the paragraphs that support the gold answers. Rebuilt over the realistic context pool (the same gold paragraphs plus their distractors, 851 chunks), the win largely collapses.

Higher token-F1 / EM = better answers; recall@5 = whether we fetched the right supporting evidence.

 
Verdict: the headline +8.8 F1 win does not survive a realistic graph. Retrieval recall still improves (recall@5 +8.2), but the extra chunks no longer convert into better answers — and the largest multi-hop slice actually regresses.

A second finding — entity resolution

The v1 entity-resolution merge precision of 0.930 was itself an artifact of the answer-enriched corpus. Under the same string-only rules, the realistic distractor-inclusive graph (full of same-name royals, families and dynasties that the dataset uses as distractors) collapsed to 0.821. Four surgical, precision-first bug-fixes restored it to 0.903 — without losing connectivity (bridge-entity coverage held at 0.907).

Why the numbers are trustworthy

Only retrieval changedGenerator, prompt, judge, metrics and frozen gold are byte-identical to the baseline — git diff over those files is empty.
DeterministicOne generation pass at temperature 0; every EM / F1 / recall@k recomputes exactly from the stored runs.
Honestly scopedRealistic graph includes distractors (no answer enrichment). Still test-question-scoped, not the full corpus — stated as future work.
